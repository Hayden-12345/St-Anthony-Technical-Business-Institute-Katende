St. Anthony Technical and Business Institute Katende is a Catholic founded institution started in 1991 as a better alternative for practical oriented learner who found academic education unattractive and for the many school drop outs in the surroundings due to civil wars during the early 70s and 80s and also as an immediate alternative basic education for the many children (who had lost parents owing to the HIV/AIDS scourge and civil wars) who found academic education rather too expensive.


St. Anthony is situated in Katende Parish, Mawokota County, Mpigi District within the central region of the Country called Buganda. It is found in Kampala Archdiocese, 20kms Kampala - Masaka road. This is in the former gazetted Luweero Triangle which was a danger zone during the wars of the 1980s which led to high maternal health related problem and infant mortality rate.


St. Anthony Technical and Business Institute was then started in order to respond to the afflicted community to empower the youth with technical and business skills. Since then, the institution under different management has consistently maintained its position in providing a wide range of skills to the youth of the area and those from other areas.


The diocese would like to use the opportunity of this institute to reach the under privileged and vulnerable groups in the society basing on Tertiary Institutions Act of Parliament (UOTIA, 2001). By the provisions of the Act, St. Anthony is mandated to provide education services like other institutions of higher learning as well as ensure delivery of quality and relevant education to all qualified persons.The institute currently offers a number of academic programmes in technical and business education at a certificate level:


The Institute developed a strategic plan for the past periods. As the plan period came to an end, it is essential to develop a new forward looking, inspirational road map to build from the achievements so far, and at the same, taking into account the environmental changes. The changing operating environment and growing stakeholdersâ€™ expectations require the Institute to enhance its quality of education and acquire in-depth research capabilities. The steady growth in student numbers has created a need for more infrastructure and facilities. Increased competition for qualified staff (a critical resource for the provision of quality education0 underscores the need to improve its capacity to attract, develop and retain staff. At the same time, the Institute needs to generate sufficient financial resources to sustain its operating expenses and development initiatives. In this background, in order to realize the vision and achieve the objectives of Tertiary education in Uganda set out by the Ministry of Education and Sports (www.education.go.ug) by effectively implementing the mission, it is imperative to develop a well thought out strategic plan. A planning horizon of five years was considered appropriate as it would allow planning and implementation of key academic research and infrastructure projects that require longer gestation period.
